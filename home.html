Why we built this

Made by a rebbi, for the way you already teach.

Menchmark started with almost a decade of substitute teaching and running school-wide programs. Walking into a different classroom every day means inheriting whatever points system the regular rebbi left behind — raffle tickets in a drawer, a clipboard no one can find, post-its peeling off desks, a chart on the board that half the class ignores. None of it carried over. None of it was mine. And running programs across multiple classrooms meant tracking dozens of students' points by hand — a job that got harder every week.

As an afternoon classroom teacher — I teach math — I saw the same problem from the other side. Every September I'd start strong — tracking attendance, homework, classwork, behavior — and by mid-year it was too overwhelming to keep up. The system would quietly collapse, and the students knew it. Saying "minus one" to a boy who knows it's not being recorded doesn't work. You lose your credibility, and with it your ability to hold the room. I needed a way to record and give feedback immediately — not at the end of the period, not after class, but right then, without friction. The research is clear: behavior changes with small, immediate feedback repeated over time. But that only works if the feedback is real — if it's actually being recorded, and the student knows it.

Every system got something right and dropped the rest. Raffle tickets gave real recognition — but they disappeared into pockets. Clipboards were portable — but invisible to students. Point charts were visible — but took the rebbi away from teaching to update them. ClassDojo was instant — but needed a smartphone, wasn't easy to set up, and took over the entire board to stay visible.

So I stopped looking for the right system and built one. I took what actually worked from each — the speed of a scan on an inexpensive handheld scanner, the visibility of a leaderboard, the tangibility of a reward store — and put it into a single tool that a rebbi can run without stopping the shiur. Rebbeim in real classrooms are testing it now, and their feedback shapes every update. It's in beta — actively being built and refined. It works on your own device, saves to your own Google Sheet, needs no student logins, and runs even when the internet doesn't. Built to serve rebbeim, not to sell them anything.

— Rabbi Berel Steinerman
